Why?

If you distribute virtual machines you often have problems like how to get an ip address (depend on DHCP) or how to provide the acquired IP-Address to your user. But for some services you not only need an ip address but also a resolvable and working hostname. As you don't have control about the used DNS infrastructure such a thing can get complicated.

Our proposed solution to this problem is to generate a resolvable hostname based on your primary ip address. This allows you to use the hostname in basically every environment without the need to either edit /etc/hosts, use MDNS or create additional DNS entries in your zone file.

How

  1. Convert your ip address into hex. Example: 192.168.42.23 -> c0a82a17
  2. Use c0a82a1.hostingstack.com as your hostname
